Defanged Horror

A collection of questionable films and their spoilers

1408 (2007)

Rating: 7/10

In 1408, a talented hotel room tries to murder a fantasy writer. After the ordeal, the writer reconciles with his estranged wife, he reconnects with his father, he writes his best-ever novel—it all seems to good to be true. And it absolutely is. He's been trapped in the room the whole time. Top notch burn. 7/10 for a gloriously elaborate and devastating ruse executed by a killer room.

Critters (1986)

Rating: 5/10

In Critters, alien bounty hunters hunt and kill tiny extraterrestrial cuties that look like bare-face Gene Simmonses. With the help of the Brown family, the cuties are defeated and there are no consequences. Everyone is fine, except hundreds—possibly thousands—of lil' cuties. 5/10 for dead cuties.

Jack Frost (1997)

Rating: 2/10

In 1998, Warner Bros released Jack Frost—a family film starring Michael Keaton as a not-great dad who dies in a car accident. He's resurrected as a snow man for some reason to make amends with his son. This Jack Frost is not that film. This film is about a serial killer who turns into a radioactive snowman to get revenge on the cop who arrested him. He's not successful, but he does murder an entire family as collateral damage. 2/10 for snowman with teeth.

Uninvited (1988)

Rating: 3/10

Sometimes people poison cats for science. Sometimes that poison alters the cats' genetic information in exactly the right way so monster cats grow inside of them. Sometimes these cats go on cruises to the Caymans with several eighties stereotypes and murder everyone onboard by vomiting up the monster cats. This is the plot of Uninvited—a film in which scantily-clad spring breakers do actually invite a mutant cat onto a yacht. 3/10 for screaming kitties.
