Defanged Horror

A collection of questionable films.

Critters (1986)

Rating: 5/10

In Critters, alien bounty hunters hunt and kill tiny extraterrestrial cuties that look like bare-face Gene Simmonses. With the help of the Brown family, the cuties are defeated and there are no consequences. Everyone is fine, except hundreds—possibly thousands—of lil' cuties. 5/10 for dead cuties.

Jack Frost (1997)

Rating: 2/10

In 1998, Warner Bros released Jack Frost—a family film starring Michael Keaton as a not-great dad who dies in a car accident. He's resurrected as a snow man for some reason to make amends with his son. This Jack Frost is not that film. This film is about a serial killer who turns into a radioactive snowman to get revenge on the cop who arrested him. He's not successful, but he does murder an entire family as collateral damage. 2/10 for snowman with teeth.

Vampire's Kiss (1989)

Rating: 8/10

Nic Cage gives the performance of a lifetime in Vampire's Kiss as an unhinged New York yuppy with a completely indiscernable accent. He shouts the alphabet. He eats a live cockroach. He murders a woman using his mouth. He runs through lower Manhattan screaming, "I AM A VAMPIRE. I AM VAMPIRE. I AM VAMPIRE." At times, he sports a Chigurh cut while arguing with Sharon, an imaginary paramore. 7/10 for peak Nic Cage.
