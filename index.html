Defanged Horror

A collection of questionable films

Critters (1986)

Rating: 5/10

In Critters, alien bounty hunters hunt and kill tiny extraterrestrial cuties that look like bare-face Gene Simmonses. With the help of the Brown family, the cuties are defeated and there are no consequences. Everyone is fine, except hundreds—possibly thousands—of lil' cuties. 5/10 for dead cuties.

Jack Frost (1997)

Rating: 2/10

In 1998, Warner Bros released Jack Frost—a family film starring Michael Keaton as a not-great dad who dies in a car accident. He's resurrected as a snow man for some reason to make amends with his son. This Jack Frost is not that film. This film is about a serial killer who turns into a radioactive snowman to get revenge on the cop who arrested him. He's not successful, but he does murder an entire family as collateral damage. 2/10 for snowman with teeth.

Uninvited (1988)

Rating: 3/10

Sometimes people poison cats for science. Sometimes that poison alters the cats' genetic information in exactly the right way so that monster cats grow inside of them. Sometimes these cats go on cruises to the Caymans with several eighties stereotypes and murder everyone onboard by vomiting up the monster cats. This is the plot of Uninvited—a film in which scantily-clad spring breakers do actually invite a mutant cat onto a yacht with a few criminals and millions of dollars. 3/10 for screaming kitties.

Vampire's Kiss (1989)

Rating: 8/10

Nic Cage gives the performance of a lifetime in Vampire's Kiss as an unhinged New York yuppy with a totally indiscernable accent. At times, he sports a Chigurh cut while arguing with Sharon, an imaginary paramore. He shouts the alphabet. He eats a live cockroach. He murders with his mouth. He runs through lower Manhattan screaming, "I AM A VAMPIRE. I AM A VAMPIRE. I AM A VAMPIRE." He terrorizes an employee over file organization. He laughs, he cries, he gags, he lights up even the smallest screen. 8/10 for career-peak Nic Cage.
